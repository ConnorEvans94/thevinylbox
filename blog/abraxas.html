Abraxas by Santana, the second studio album released by the american band. The first Santana album to reach No.1 in the US. Released 23rd September 1970 by Columbia records and produced by Fred Catero and Carlos Santana. The band recorded the album at Wally Heider recording studio in San Francisco where the band is from, they formed in 1966 with the first album "santana" being released in 1969. Carlos Santana was on the lead guitar, backing vocals and producer. Gregg Rolie was on the keyboard and lead vocals. David Brown on the bass. Michael Shrieve on drums. Jose 'Chepito' Areas on percussion, conga and timbales. Michael Carabello on percussion and conga. The album is named after a god in the book DEMIAN by Hermann Hesse, the excerpt reads: "We stood before it began to freeze inside from the exertion. We questioned the painting, berated it, made love to it, prayed to it: We called it mother, called it whore and slut, called it our beloved, called it Abraxas...."
